December 6, 2025

First Successful Model Rocket Launch at Homestead Public Rocketry Club Monthly Launch

With the help of former Lourdes Physics teacher Rusty Mayo and Aerospace Aficionado Sergio Cruz, the Ad Astra team attempted our first ever model rocket launches. We launched the Estes ready to fly kit rockets Rascal and Hi-Jinks on C motors, but the star of the show was the beloved original Big Bertha, assembled as the group's first ever model rocket. She had a 100% success rate at this event, launching on 2 C motors and 1 D motor.

February 7th, 2026

Ad Astra Attends Second Homestead Launch

Our second launch, being much more experimental and held on a windier day than the first, did not achieve the same success rate, yet it provided significant insight to many of the variables that aerospace engineers must account for in their designs and developmental processes. Big Bertha was tragically lost, carried into a nearby military compound by the wind after a successful D motor launch, while Bigger Bertha entered the lineup, burning through its shock cord with an E motor and landing partially in a tree. No egg was harmed in the process.

March 7th, 2026

Ad Astra Attends Yet Another Homestead Launch for the Threepeat

As a bittersweet ending to the club's inaugural ARC season, some new faces were seen at this launch, while the Nationals qualifying flight became just out of reach for us. Our now painted frontrunner Bigger Bertha landed in a tree after a successful launch, removing the flight's potential to be counted as a qualifier. The rocket sustained damaged upon impact in the tree and its subsequent launches were unsuccessful.

Several members took their passion for aerospace and joined together to attend the Artemis II official launch in Cape Canaveral, only a 3 hour drive north from our home base in Miami. Even though they had an unforgettable experience, they undeniably felt the void created by the absence of their renowned, conventionally and unconventionally attractive Co-President Jorge Manuel "Ryan Gosling" Ros, who inconceivably contracted Influenza B and COVID-19 at the same time, suffering brutal mind-numbing headaches and debilitating fatigue. Ad Astra
Who We Are

We are engineers, makers, and dreamers building the future of aerospace from a high school classroom.

Ad Astra is a student-led aerospace engineering club dedicated to hands-on learning through real engineering challenges. We design, build, test, and launch rockets from scratch.

Founded in 2025, our mission is simple: give students the tools, knowledge, and experience to pursue careers in aerospace, engineering, and the sciences before they ever set foot on a college campus.

We compete in national challenges including TARC (The American Rocketry Challenge). Our work is documented, rigorous, and taken seriously.

Our current executive board at a launch.

01 — Mission
Build Real Things
Every project results in hardware that flies, is tested, or solves a real engineering problem. No theory without practice.
02 — Mission
Learn Through Failure
We conduct post-flight analyses, design reviews, and failure investigations, just like professional aerospace teams.
03 — Mission
Compete Nationally
We enter national competitions to benchmark our work against the best student teams in the country.
04 — Mission
Inspire Others
We host open lab days, mentor middle school students, and show that aerospace is accessible to everyone.

TARC 2025 — Bigger Bertha

A single-stage high-power rocket designed for the American Rocketry Challenge. Target: 750ft apogee with a raw egg payload returned intact. Airframe uses fiberglass layup over foam mandrel with carbon fiber reinforced fin can.

OpenAlt — Custom Altimeter

Open-source dual-deployment altimeter board built around the STM32F4 microcontroller and BMP390 barometric sensor. Features datalogging, redundant e-match firing circuits, and a compact 29mm form factor for minimum-diameter vehicles.

Prometheus — L2 High Power Rocket

Next-generation 4" diameter high-power rocket targeting 5,000ft on a J-class motor. This vehicle will carry our OpenAlt avionics package and serve as the club's L2 certification vehicle for senior members in 2025.

AeroViz — Flight Simulation Suite

In-house Python simulation framework built on OpenRocket API outputs. Adds Monte Carlo dispersion analysis, six-degree-of-freedom trajectory modeling, and automatic stability margin calculations for our vehicle designs.

Icarus I — Certification Launch

The club's first L1 certification rocket. 2.5" diameter, 38mm motor mount, dual-deployment parachute recovery. Successfully flown October 28, 2024 to 312m apogee. All recovery systems nominal. Egg payload survived.

A miniaturized atmospheric research payload designed to profile temperature, humidity, and pressure during ascent. Data will be downlinked via LoRa radio. Intended to fly as secondary payload aboard Prometheus in late 2025.

TO THE
STARS

A long-term vision for what Ad Astra will become — semester by semester, launch by launch.

SEM 1 · 2024
Foundation & First Flight

Club chartered, leadership elected, first L1 certification achieved. Icarus I flies. Fabrication partnership established. AeroViz development begins.

DONE

2026
NASA Student Launch Initiative

Apply and compete in NASA SLI — the nation's premier student rocketry program. Design a 10,000ft research rocket with a science payload selected through NASA's review process.

2027+
Sounding Rocket & CubeSat

Long-term: develop a two-stage sounding rocket and pursue a CubeSat mission through NASA CubeSat Launch Initiative. Partner with local universities for research payloads and mentorship.

What Drives Us

The Vision

🚀
Real Hardware

We build vehicles that leave the ground. Every semester, members work on hardware that tests, flies, and returns data. Engineering is learned by doing.

🛰️
Space Access

Our long-term trajectory points to space. A CubeSat in low Earth orbit — designed and built entirely by high school students — is not a dream. It's a plan.

🔬
Original Research

Ad Astra will publish open flight data, atmospheric profiles, and hardware documentation so other student teams can build on our work.

🏆
National Competition

We aim to place in the top 10 at TARC nationals and earn a spot in the NASA Student Launch Initiative within three years of founding.

🤝
Community Pipeline

We mentor middle school students through launch day events and STEM nights — building the next generation of aerospace engineers before they reach high school.

📡
Open Source

All schematics, code, and design files are published under open licenses. What we build should benefit every student team that comes after us.
